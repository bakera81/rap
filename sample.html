About “Mask Off”

“Mask Off” is a cut off Future’s self-titled album that somewhat functions as a “rags-to-riches” story that explores the surface of Future’s past, from trapping to living a life of luxury.

The slow and syrupy production by Metro Boomin samples the 1976 song, “Prison Song” by Tommy Butler, which featured Carlton Williams. The melancholic tune may be an indictment of mass incarceration, but the Future’s emergent riches insulate him from the punishment that is unfailingly administered to countless of men living in the despair. A religious message can be heard in the sample before Future’s second verse. This seems to belie extravagant lifestyle flaunted in this record.

“Mask Off” went on to become Future’s highest-charting single ever, peaking at #5 on the Billboard Hot 100. The song was also certified 5X platinum by the RIAA.

The #MaskOffChallenge, a social media craze where participants dance and play instruments to the song, was a huge factor in the song’s popularity.

The music video was released on May 4, 2017. It also features cameos from Amber Rose.

On May 23, 2017, the official remix for “Mask Off” was released, which featured Kendrick Lamar.

  • What is the sample?

    Frank Dukes provided the sample of a musical record from the 70’s by Tommy Butler, called “Prison Song”. The sampled song is from The “Selma” Album, a musical tribute to Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr.

  • What is the #MaskOffChallenge?

    The #MaskOffChallenge is people dancing and playing instruments to the song. It became instantly popular because of the flute-inspired beat and catchy hook of the song.

    Even Chris Brown had some moves for this song.
